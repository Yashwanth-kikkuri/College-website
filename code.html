ABOUT US

At Code Club, we think all students should have the opportunity to learn to code, no matter who they are or where they come from.

Our projects are easy to follow, step-by-step guides which help young people learn Scratch, HTML & CSS, and Python by making games, animations, and websites. The projects gradually introduce coding concepts to allow young people to build their knowledge incrementally, which also means there's no need for the adult running the session to be a computing expert.

We also support active clubs with a range of extra resources, including certificates and posters, as well as offering competitions and prize draws for them.

HACKATHON

HACKATHON this year will be focusing on urban water crisis, both in terms of quantity and quality. Since what cannot be measured cannot be improved, we will concentrate on designing and demonstrating innovative low cost tools to measure and monitor water parameters with appropriate accuracy. We should be able to measure both supply and demand, gap between them, quality of the water being supplied, energy needed for it, etc. E.g. Rainfall, ground water level, reservoir water level, number of taps, consumption per tap, water quality (turbidity, salinity, etc.), energy consumption, etc. Stakeholders should be able to visualize and analyze the problem and design and implement solutions. Latest IoT and web technologies are making this possible.

During the HACKATHON, participants will form 3-6 member teams. Each team will be given a microcontroller (e.g. Raspberry Pi 3), a few sensors, access to the internet, etc. They will need to design, develop and demonstrate innovative solutions to these challenges. They will be mentored by industry experts. For more details, please write to us at research_expo@iiits.in or call us on 7337 324904, 9100 190811.
